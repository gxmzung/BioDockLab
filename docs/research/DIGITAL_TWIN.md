# Digital Twin Research Direction

## Overview

Digital twin is one of the core long-term directions of BioDockLab.

In this project, digital twin means a software-based simulation layer that connects experiment conditions, AI analysis, and predicted biological responses.

BioDockLab does not claim biological or clinical validation at this stage.  
The current goal is to explore how experiment data can be structured, simulated, compared, and summarized through software.

---

## Concept Flow

```text
Experiment Condition
        ↓
AI Analysis
        ↓
Predicted Biological Response
        ↓
Risk / Success Estimation
        ↓
Research Report
Why Digital Twin Matters

Biological experiments often require time, cost, materials, and repeated trials.

A digital twin-style simulation layer can help researchers:

Compare experimental conditions
Estimate possible response changes
Review risk or success tendency
Prepare better follow-up experiments
Generate structured research reports
BioDockLab Integration

Digital twin logic can be connected to:

Experiment records
Risk classification
Success rate analysis
Organoid response simulation
CFPS yield simulation
Research report generation
Example Parameters

Possible simulation inputs:

Treatment strength
Risk score
Reaction time
Temperature
Sample state
Condition value
Previous experiment result

Possible simulation outputs:

Predicted response score
Estimated risk level
Simulation result summary
Recommended adjustment
Report-ready explanation
Current Status

Current stage:

Function-level simulation prototype

Digital twin workflow planning

Not implemented yet:

Real biological validation
```
